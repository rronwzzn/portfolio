## FOCUSING ON HIV-POSITIVE REGIONS TO GIVE MEDICAL ADVICE AND TREATMENT

This project utilizes Pandas, GeoPandas, and Folium to analyze, visualize, and interpret data regarding HIV occurrences across different regions in the Philippines to help guide actionable healthcare strategies.

---

## Project Overview

The problem tackled in this study is identifying exactly which region or regions in the Philippines have the highest occurrences of HIV cases based on 2022 dataset trends. By utilizing Python and its data science libraries, this project extracts and analyzes data made available through government and public repositories. 

By examining the relationship between regional case counts and the locations of established testing centers, the study determines how healthcare resources align with regional numbers. Through geospatial visualization and regional comparison, the project highlights potential gaps in medical advice and treatment availability, ultimately contributing to targeted mitigation and healthcare resource allocation.

---

## Objectives

* Analyze internal datasets to determine total HIV-positive case generation per region.
* Rank and identify the top regions with the highest occurrences of cases.
* Standardize Coordinate Reference Systems (CRS) to merge regional data with geospatial maps.
* Visualize data using choropleth maps, heatmaps, and markers for HIV testing centers.
* Provide data-driven insights to bridge the gap between data availability and actionable healthcare strategies.

---

---

## Expected Outcome

The project provides a clearer, visual understanding of how HIV cases are distributed across the regions of the Philippines, explicitly isolating high-density areas like the National Capital Region (NCR) and CALABARZON. 

The interactive maps and graphs produced serve as an analytical tool to guide medical workers, environmental planners, and local health departments in deploying targeted medical advice, treatments, and testing facility expansions where they are needed most.
