This study developed an automated computer vision system that verifies the personal protective equipment (PPE) compliance of construction workers in pre-entry and on-site areas. The system integrates a YOLOv11-based object detection model with dual camera inputs to identify the presence or absence of key PPE components: hard hats, safety vests, gloves, and safety shoes. A dataset of 5,105 images was collected, annotated, and augmented, producing 15,818 training images. The model was trained and validated using Roboflow and Ultralytics YOLOv11s.The system provides real-time detection, visual bounding boxes, and audio-visual alerts for non-compliance. Testing in a simulated construction environment shows that the system achieved high mean average accuracy and speed across different lighting conditions and distances. The proposed system offers a reliable and efficient solution to enhance safety monitoring, reduce manual inspection limitations, and improve overall safety compliance on construction sites. 

Keywords: computer vision, PPE compliance, YOLOv11, construction safety, deep learning
